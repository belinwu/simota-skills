# Topic DNA

## Purpose

"What are you known for?" is the brand question every engineer eventually faces. Topic DNA — the intersection of Tech × Domain × Perspective — answers it. A defined Topic DNA gives every profile, post, talk, and interview a unifying axis. Without one, the engineer is forgettable in a crowded market.

## Scope Boundary

- IN scope: positioning statement authoring, Tech × Domain × Perspective triangulation, content-pillar derivation, defensibility audit, durability check, audience-fit verification.
- OUT of scope: SNS publishing strategy (`sns`), content calendar / amplification (`blog`), profile copy on a specific platform (`github` / `linkedin` / `bio`), portfolio architecture (`portfolio`), full-funnel marketing (delegate to `growth` / `funnel`).

## Core Concepts

### The Triangulation

A defensible niche sits at the intersection of three axes:

| Axis | Question | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| Tech | What do you build with? | Rust, TypeScript, Postgres, Kubernetes, ML, Game engine |
| Domain | What domain do you build for? | Fintech, biotech, devtools, climate, music, accessibility |
| Perspective | What is your distinctive angle? | Performance obsessive, accessibility-first, indie-hacker, security-first, teaching-driven, OSS maintainer |

A pure-Tech identity ("Rust engineer") is replaceable. A pure-Domain identity ("fintech engineer") is generic. A pure-Perspective identity ("performance obsessive") is too vague. The Tech × Domain × Perspective intersection is where credibility compounds.

|----------|------|--------|-------------|------------------------|
| Linus Torvalds | C, kernel | Operating systems | Pragmatic curmudgeon | "Open-source kernel maintainer with a sharp tongue and zero patience for stupidity." |
| Sara Soueidan | HTML, CSS, SVG | Web | Accessibility-first | "SVG and accessibility expert teaching the web to be more humane." |
| Brad Frost | HTML, CSS, design-system tooling | Web | Atomic systems | "Design-system advocate with a method for building scalable component libraries." |

| Wes Bos | JavaScript, web | Education | Tutorial-driven | "JS teacher producing premium courses on modern web stacks." |

Crisp Topic DNA is identifiable in one sentence and survives a 5-second elevator pitch.

### Defensibility Audit

A Topic DNA must be:

| Test | Question |
|------|----------|
| Specific | Can someone repeat it back accurately after one read? |
| Defensible | Are there fewer than 100 engineers globally who hold this exact intersection? |
| Demonstrable | Can you point to 3+ public artifacts (repos / posts / talks) that prove it? |
| Audible | When others describe you, do they use words from your Topic DNA? |
| Durable | Will this still be relevant in 12 months? In 3 years? |
| Genuine | Do you actually want to do this work, or are you chasing a trend? |

If all six pass, the Topic DNA is shipping-ready.

### Durability vs Trend

Avoid Topic DNA tied to single technologies that age fast:

| Risk | Trend | Durable Reframe |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| High | "Web3 NFT engineer" (2021) | "Distributed systems with cryptographic verification" |
| High | "Prompt engineering specialist" (2024) | "AI evaluation and reliability for production systems" |
| Medium | "Tailwind CSS expert" | "CSS architecture for maintainable design systems" |
| Low | "TypeScript performance optimization" | (the work and language are stable) |

Tech component should be a class of problem, not a hype tool.

### Content Pillars from Topic DNA

Once Topic DNA is set, content automatically derives:

| Topic DNA | Pillar 1 | Pillar 2 | Pillar 3 | Pillar 4 |
|-----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| "Postgres performance for fintech" | Index design | Query plans | Replication / HA | Compliance & audit |
| "TypeScript DX in monorepos" | Type system depth | Tooling (ts-config, build) | Editor / LSP integration | Migration patterns |
| "Accessibility-first React" | ARIA patterns | Keyboard / focus | Screen-reader testing | Color & contrast tooling |

Aim for **3–5 pillars**. Each pillar generates years of articles, talks, and projects.

### The 80/20 Rule

LinkedIn's 360Brew Topic DNA model recommends:

- **80%** of content within Topic DNA pillars (depth signal).
- **20%** outside the pillars (breadth signal — keeps you human).

Below 80% on-pillar dilutes positioning. Above 95% becomes one-note.

### Audience Fit

Topic DNA must intersect a real audience:

| Audience size | Signal |
|---------------|--------|
| < 1,000 globally | Niche of niche; possibly too narrow unless fee per client is high |
| 1,000–10,000 | Healthy expert niche |
| 10,000–100,000 | Strong niche; can sustain courses, books, conferences |
| 100,000+ | Mass-market expertise; harder to differentiate |

Smaller niches monetize per-client at higher rates; larger niches at scale via products. Pick based on monetization preference.

### Common Failure Modes

| Failure | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Buzzword soup | "AI-powered cloud-native serverless TypeScript engineer" | Pick one Tech, one Domain, one Perspective |
| Pure resume | "Senior software engineer with 10 years experience" | Move from history to current obsession |
| Trend-tied | "AI agent prompt engineer" | Reframe to durable skill class |
| Brand-dependent | "Ex-Google engineer" | Insufficient — what do you DO? |
| Vague perspective | "Engineer who cares about quality" | What kind of quality? performance? a11y? security? |
| Over-broad | "Full-stack engineer" | Too generic for positioning; reserve for skills section |
| Aspirational | "Future founder building the next Stripe" | Build the work first, brand the work second |

### Triangulation Worksheet

A practical exercise:

1. List 5 technologies you've shipped non-trivial work in (last 24 months).
2. List 3 domains you've worked in or have credible interest in.
3. List 5 perspectives that describe how you approach problems differently.
4. Form 5 candidate triangulations: Tech[i] × Domain[j] × Perspective[k].
5. Run defensibility audit on each.
6. Pick top 1–2 and write the one-sentence positioning.

### Two-Tier Strategy

Some engineers maintain:

- **Primary Topic DNA** — used in headline, About, conference bios.
- **Secondary Topic DNA** — used for side-projects, experiments, hobby.

Two-tier is fine if both are clearly demarcated. The risk is signal dilution if both appear at primary positions.

### Evolution Cadence

Topic DNA should evolve every **18–36 months**, not quarterly. Stability builds credibility; constant rebranding signals confusion.

| Trigger to update | Healthy |
|-------------------|---------|
| Major career change (role, industry) | yes |
| Public artifact corpus shifted significantly | yes |
| Audience grew beyond original niche | yes |
| You stopped enjoying the work | yes |
| Trend is fading | yes (re-anchor on durable skill) |
| New tool dropped this week | no |
| Quarterly "rebrand" cycle | no |

## Workflow

1. **List 5 tech axes / 3 domains / 5 perspectives** from real shipped work.
2. **Form 5 candidate triangulations**.
3. **Draft a one-sentence positioning** per candidate.
4. **Run defensibility audit** — 6 checks (specific / defensible / demonstrable / audible / durable / genuine).
5. **Audience-fit check** — niche size estimate.
6. **Derive 3–5 content pillars** from the chosen Topic DNA.
7. **Apply 80/20 ratio plan** for content allocation.
8. **Document Topic DNA** in a single canonical artifact (used to seed bio, profile, talks).
9. **Set evolution review** for 18–36 months out.
10. **Hand off** to `bio` for platform variants, `portfolio` for site IA, `blog` / `sns` / `conference` for content amplification.

## Anti-Patterns

- Buzzword soup — combining 4+ technologies in the headline.
- Trend-bound Tech axis — dies with the trend.
- Pure resume framing — past, not present.
- Vague Perspective ("quality-focused", "team player").
- Topic DNA that doesn't match what you actually do daily.
- Aspirational DNA before any artifacts back it up.
- Quarterly rebranding — no compounding.
- Two-tier with unclear demarcation — confuses everyone.
- Niche under 1,000 globally without high fee-per-client model.
- Niche over 100,000 with no differentiation — invisible.
- Topic DNA derived from competitor envy rather than genuine interest.
- Topic DNA that requires explanation longer than one sentence.
- Pillars overlapping — same content categorized two ways.
- Pillars below 3 (too narrow) or above 5 (too scattered).
- 100% on-pillar content — robotic, no humanity signal.
- Below 70% on-pillar — positioning collapses.

## Deliverable Contract

A Topic DNA package is complete when:

- One-sentence positioning written.
- Tech / Domain / Perspective named explicitly.
- Defensibility audit passes 6/6 checks.
- Audience size band estimated.
- 3–5 content pillars derived with share % summing to 100.
- 80/20 on-pillar policy stated.
- Review date set 18–36 months out.
- Handoff briefs prepared for `bio`, `portfolio`, `blog`, `sns`, `conference`.
