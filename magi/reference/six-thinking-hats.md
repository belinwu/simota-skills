# Six Thinking Hats Reference

Purpose: Edward de Bono's parallel-thinking protocol that separates a single deliberation into six discrete cognitive modes (one "hat" at a time, the whole group wears the same hat together). Replaces argumentative cross-talk with sequenced single-mode passes, surfacing facts, feelings, risks, benefits, alternatives, and process control without the role-fixation that plagues classic debate.

## Scope Boundary

- **magi `sixhat`**: parallel-thinking protocol applied to a proposal already on the table. Group cycles through six hats in a planned sequence. Output is a hat-by-hat synthesis feeding the standard Logos/Pathos/Sophia VOTE.
- **magi `decide` / `tradeoff` / `arbitrate` / `strategic` (default lanes)**: independent three-perspective evaluation. Use when a structured verdict is the goal, not divergent surfacing.
- **magi `devil` (elsewhere)**: formal opposition. Black Hat is one mode of six; Devil's Advocate is an entire structured red-team protocol with rotated dissenter, not a single-mode pass.
- **magi `delphi` (elsewhere)**: anonymous multi-round expert convergence. Six Hats is single-room, named, parallel — not anonymous, not iterative across days.
- **flux (elsewhere)**: reframes the problem (axes, lenses, paradigm shifts). Six Hats keeps the problem fixed and rotates evaluation modes.
- **riff (elsewhere)**: iterative dialogue idea-deepening. Six Hats is single-pass per hat with strict mode discipline.
- **omen (elsewhere)**: enumerates failure modes via FMEA/RPN. Black Hat overlaps but is qualitative, time-boxed, and embedded in a six-mode rotation.

## Workflow

```
SETUP    →  facilitator picks Blue Hat opener; selects sequence (eval vs solve)
         →  state proposal in one sentence; agree time box per hat (3-5 min typical)

BLUE-1   →  define purpose, scope, success criteria for this session
         →  declare hat order; participants commit to mode discipline

ROTATE   →  whole group wears same hat at the same time
         →  no cross-hat interjections; out-of-mode comments parked by Blue

CAPTURE  →  scribe logs each hat's outputs as separate columns (W/R/B/Y/G)
         →  no confidence scores yet; surface-mode only

BLUE-2   →  summarize, choose action, route to Magi VOTE or extend a hat
         →  if Yellow+Green dominate Black: green-light; else loop
```

## The Six Hats

| Hat | Color | Mode | Prompt | Anti-mode (caution) |
|-----|-------|------|--------|---------------------|
| White | White | Facts and information | What do we know? What's missing? | Speculation, interpretation |
| Red | Red | Emotion, intuition, gut | How does this feel? (no justification) | Rationalizing, defending |
| Black | Black | Caution, risk, critical judgment | What could go wrong? What's the weakness? | Personal attack, blanket negativity |
| Yellow | Yellow | Optimism, value, benefits | What's the upside? Why will it work? | Wishful thinking without basis |
| Green | Green | Creativity, alternatives, new ideas | What else? What if? | Editing or judging ideas |
| Blue | Blue | Process, meta, control | What hat next? Are we on track? | Acting in another hat's mode |

## Sequencing Protocols

| Sequence | Use case | Order | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|-------|
| Idea-evaluation | Assessing a single concrete proposal | Blue → White → Yellow → Black → Red → Blue | Facts first, then upside, then risk, gut last |
| Problem-solving | Open-ended challenge, no proposal yet | Blue → White → Green → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue | Generate before judging; second Green addresses Black's risks |
| Quick decision | Time-boxed Go/No-Go (under 15 min) | Blue → Black → Yellow → Red → Blue | Risk and value, then gut check |
| Conflict resolution | Group is stuck or polarized | Blue → Red → White → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue | Surface emotion first, then ground in facts |
| Strategic planning | Long-horizon decisions | Blue → Yellow → Black → White → Green → Blue | Vision, risks, evidence, alternatives |

## Time-Boxing Defaults

| Hat | Default | Min | Max | Rationale |
|-----|---------|-----|-----|-----------|
| Blue | 2 min | 1 | 5 | Process should be tight |
| White | 4 min | 3 | 8 | Fact recall takes time |
| Red | 1 min | 0.5 | 2 | Gut is fast; long Red invites rationalization |
| Black | 5 min | 3 | 10 | Risk surfacing benefits from depth |
| Yellow | 4 min | 3 | 8 | Symmetric with Black to prevent imbalance |
| Green | 5 min | 3 | 10 | Creativity needs room |

Total typical session: 20-35 min for evaluation, 30-50 min for problem-solving.

## Hat-Switching Rules

- **Whole group, same hat**: never split (e.g., "you take Black, I take Yellow") — that's role-fixation, not parallel thinking.
- **No cross-hat in mode**: a Black comment during Yellow is parked by Blue and surfaced when Black returns.
- **Blue owns transitions**: only the facilitator (Blue) calls the next hat; participants don't self-switch.
- **Symmetry rule**: every Black session must be paired with a Yellow session of equal time, to prevent bias toward criticism or optimism.
- **Red is brief and unjustified**: Red feelings are stated, not defended. "I feel uneasy" stands; "I feel uneasy because X" is Black in disguise.
- **Green is judgment-free**: ideas float without evaluation; evaluation happens only when Black/Yellow return.

## Facilitator (Blue Hat) Script

- Opening: "We're deciding [proposal] in [time]. We'll use [sequence]. Hat order: [list]. I'll call hats and time-keep."
- Mid-session: "We're now wearing [Hat]. The question is [hat's prompt]. Out-of-mode contributions get parked."
- Park: "Good point — I'll park that as a [Black/Yellow/Green] item for when we get there."
- Closing Blue: "Summary: [White facts] + [Yellow benefits] + [Black risks] + [Red gut] + [Green alternatives]. Recommended action: [verdict]."

## Tie-In With Magi's Three Perspectives

- White outputs feed **Logos** (facts, evidence base).
- Red and Pathos overlap (emotion, human impact) — Red is collective gut; Pathos is structured stakeholder modeling.
- Black informs **Logos risk register** and **Pathos team-impact concerns**.
- Yellow and Green inform **Sophia** (business value, alternatives).
- Blue maps to Magi's facilitator role at SYNTHESIZE.
- After Six Hats: pass hat outputs as evidence into the standard FRAME → DELIBERATE → VOTE workflow. Six Hats produces structured surfacings; Magi produces the verdict.

## Anti-Patterns

- Splitting hats across people ("you're Black, I'm Yellow") — that's classic debate with color labels, not parallel thinking. Group must wear the same hat together.
- Skipping the second Blue — without a closing Blue, outputs scatter and no decision is named.
- Over-running Black without matching Yellow — produces a critique session masquerading as deliberation; pairs of equal time.
- Letting Red turn into argued opinion — "I feel X because Y, Z, and W" is Black in red costume. Cap Red at 60-90 seconds, no justification required.
- Using Six Hats on a problem with no proposal — switch to problem-solving sequence (Green-heavy) or run Flux first to reframe.
- Treating Six Hats as a verdict mechanism — it's a surfacing tool. Always feed outputs into Magi's VOTE phase for the actual decision.
- Running Six Hats with one person — it's designed for parallel group thinking; solo use collapses into ordinary checklist review and loses the parallelism benefit.
- Mixing hats in a single round (Black-then-Yellow ping-pong) — destroys mode discipline; use the planned sequence and hold the line.

## Handoff

- **To Magi VOTE**: hat-column outputs become structured evidence for Logos (White, Black-risks), Pathos (Red, Black-team-impact), Sophia (Yellow, Green).
- **To Flux**: if Green Hat outputs reveal the original frame is wrong, halt and route to Flux for reframing before resuming.
- **To Omen**: Black Hat outputs that warrant quantitative scoring (RPN/AP) escalate to Omen for FMEA-grade enumeration.
- **To Riff**: if Green Hat surfaces an idea worth deepening rather than deciding, route to Riff for iterative expansion before returning to Magi.
- **To human**: if Blue closing reveals no consensus and Red gut-checks diverge sharply from Black/Yellow analysis, escalate as a values disagreement, not a fact dispute.
