# Career Frameworks

Route comparison, positioning, personal brand, and elevator-pitch frameworks. Load for `direction` and positioning work.

## Route Comparison

Compare at least two of: **Stay & grow**, **Job change**, **Side-business**, **Independence**.

Score each route on a 1–5 scale across these axes, then recommend one:

| Axis | Question |
|------|----------|
| Income continuity | Does income persist during the transition? |
| Reversibility | Can the user undo this within ~3 months? |
| Timeline fit | Does it match the user's stated timeline? |
| Skill leverage | Does it use existing strengths/achievements? |
| Market demand | Is there sourced demand for the target? |
| Risk tolerance fit | Does downside match the user's stated tolerance? |

Output: a comparison table, the recommended route, the rationale, and the **trade-offs the user is accepting** by choosing it. Never collapse to one route without showing what was given up.

Irreversible-risk gate: quitting before securing income, relocation, visa change, or large financial commitment → flag and Ask First before recommending.

## Positioning Statement (canonical)

One sentence drives every asset:

> For **[target audience/employer]** who need **[need]**, I am the **[role/archetype]** who **[unique value]**, proven by **[evidence/achievement]**.

Derive the resume headline, LinkedIn/profile summary, elevator pitch, and self-intro scripts from this single statement so all assets stay consistent.

## Personal Brand (labor-market, not channel)

Triangulate: **Skill × Domain × Perspective**. This is the *person-in-market* differentiation — distinct from Crest's channel/algorithm branding (GitHub/blog/SNS). If the user is an engineer wanting channel content strategy, hand off to Crest.

## Elevator Pitch

Three lengths from one positioning statement:
- 10-second: role + unique value.
- 30-second: + one proof point + what you're looking for.
- 2-minute: + brief arc (where you came from → what you do now → where you're going).
