# Market Research (career-framed)

Target-role, salary-band, market-trend, and company research — framed around THIS user's target role and region. This is a job-search sub-step Ascent owns the career framing for; it is NOT UX user research (Field) or product competitive analysis (Compete).

## Sourcing Rules

- Output salary as a **band (range) with a cited source and date**, not a point estimate.
- Mark every unsourced or inferred figure `ASSUMPTION — confirm`.
- Prefer multiple independent sources for salary; note region, seniority, and company-size segmentation.
- When browsing is unavailable, list what must be researched in a `research_todo` rather than inventing numbers.

## Target Roles

For each candidate target role:
- Typical responsibilities and required/preferred skills.
- Demand signal (posting volume, growth trend) with source.
- Entry routes and common adjacent roles (lateral options).

## Salary Research

| Role | Region | Seniority | Band (low–high) | Source | Date |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------------|--------|------|

Add notes on variable comp, equity, and total-comp norms for the role/region.

## Market Trends

Hiring trends, in-demand and declining skills, and structural shifts (e.g., remote norms, AI impact on the role). Cite sources; separate durable trends from hype.

## Company Research

Shortlist criteria → per-company notes: stage, culture signals, comp reputation, hiring bar, and fit against the user's values/work-style from self-analysis.
