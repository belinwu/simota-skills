# Brand Equity — Keller's CBBE Pyramid

Purpose: measure and build the strength of a brand from the customer's point of view —
*how much the brand itself* (beyond product features and price) drives preference, premium,
and loyalty. Keller's Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) model gives a four-level ladder
from "do they know us?" to "are they loyal advocates?".

Use this when assessing brand strength vs competitors, diagnosing why a strong product
under-converts, or planning a brand-building program. Pairs with competitive positioning
(`competitive-moats-category-design.md`) and brand visibility tracking.

> **Scope note.** CBBE measures *product/company brand equity*. For narrative/voice
> (`saga`), persona archetypes (`cast`), or personal/engineer branding (`crest`), route
> there instead — this file is the competitive brand-strength lens.

## The CBBE Pyramid (bottom → top)

Brand equity is built level by level; you cannot skip a level.

| Level | Question | Building block | Goal |
|-------|----------|----------------|------|
| 1. `Salience` (identity) | "Who are you?" | brand awareness — breadth & depth of recall | be the brand that comes to mind for the category/need |
| 2. `Performance` + `Imagery` (meaning) | "What are you?" | functional performance + psychological/social imagery | establish what the brand does and what it stands for |
| 3. `Judgments` + `Feelings` (response) | "What about you?" | quality/credibility/superiority judgments + emotional response | earn positive, rational *and* emotional reactions |
| 4. `Resonance` (relationships) | "What about you and me?" | loyalty, attachment, community, active engagement | the apex — the hardest and most valuable level |

- **Left side (rational route):** Salience → Performance → Judgments → Resonance.
- **Right side (emotional route):** Salience → Imagery → Feelings → Resonance.
  A strong brand climbs *both* sides.

## Measuring Each Level

| Level | Indicative metrics |
|-------|--------------------|
| Salience | aided/unaided awareness, recall, **share of voice** (see `geo-optimization.md` for AI-answer share of voice) |
| Performance/Imagery | attribute ratings, brand-association surveys, perceived-quality scores |
| Judgments/Feelings | brand consideration, Net Promoter, sentiment analysis (route raw feedback to `Voice`) |
| Resonance | repeat purchase, retention/LTV (`pulse`/`bond`), advocacy, community activity, **brand lift** studies |

A brand can be strong at the base (high awareness) but stall mid-pyramid — that diagnosis
("known but not preferred") is the model's main practical payoff.

## Using It Competitively
- Plot your brand *and* key competitors on the pyramid: a competitor weak at Resonance but
  strong at Salience is vulnerable to a loyalty/community play; one strong at Resonance has a
  defensible moat (link to `moat-7-powers.md` — Branding power).
- Brand equity is one of the **7 Powers** (Branding); CBBE is how you measure that power's
  depth. Feed the diagnosis into positioning (`competitive-moats-category-design.md`).

## Handoffs
- Raw customer feedback / sentiment / NPS collection → `Voice`.
- Retention / loyalty / LTV instrumentation → `pulse` (`metrics-frameworks.md`) and `bond`.
- AI-answer-engine share of voice → `growth` (`geo-optimization.md`).
- Narrative & messaging that expresses the brand → `saga` / `Prose`.
- Defensibility framing of brand as a moat → `moat-7-powers.md`.
